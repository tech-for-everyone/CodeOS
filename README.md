# DevOS

DevOS is a modern operating system built for bleeding-edge programmers who demand a streamlined, fast, and deeply customizable development environment. From the kernel up, DevOS is engineered to make experimentation effortless, integrate advanced tooling, and remove friction from building next-generation software.


Whether you’re exploring new languages, pushing hardware to its limits, or rapidly prototyping ambitious ideas, DevOS provides a minimal, developer-first foundation that stays out of your way while giving you full control.


# Features


Developer-First Design – Built from the ground up for programmers, with sane defaults, minimal bloat, and a focus on productivity.

Highly Customizable Environment – Tune everything from the shell and window manager to low-level system behavior to match your exact workflow.

Optimized for Performance – Lean system services, fast boot times, and low overhead so more of your hardware is available for builds, tests, and experiments.

Advanced Tooling Integration – First-class support for modern languages, debuggers, profilers, and build systems out of the box.

Experimentation-Friendly – Designed to make it easy to spin up, tear down, and isolate experimental setups without polluting your main environment.

Hardware-Tinker Ready – Ideal for pushing devices to their limits, benchmarking, and exploring low-level system behavior.

Evolving Platform – Rapid iteration and frequent updates focused on the needs of bleeding-edge developers.


# Status

Status: Early in development — expect rapid changes, frequent updates, and potential breaking changes as DevOS evolves.


# Getting Started

DevOS is still in an experimental phase and currently targeted at advanced users who are comfortable with low-level systems work.




# Check Requirements

Ensure you have compatible hardware and are prepared to dedicate a test machine or VM. DevOS is not yet recommended for daily-driver use.
